LIGHTHOUSE OF ALEXANDRIA

~Its light cut through storms and shadows — a signal that civilization awaited beyond the waves.~

“Even in ruin, it lit the imaginations of all who dreamed of reaching distant lands.”

“Where sky met sea, the Pharos stood — not just a tower, but a promise.”

By Philip Galle

The Lighthouse of Alexandria, built around 280 B.C. on the small island of Pharos, was one of the tallest man-made structures of the ancient world, guiding sailors safely into the busy port of Alexandria, Egypt. Designed by Sostratus of Cnidus, though he cleverly hid his name in the foundation under a layer of plaster bearing the king's name.

Built with three tiers: a square base, an octagonal midsection, and a cylindrical top. Used mirrors (possibly polished bronze) to project sunlight during the day. At night, it was lit by an open flame, making it visible up to 50 km away.

By Tolga TEZCAN

By Guillaume Seignac

By Qasr Libya Museum

From Kitab al-bulhan

Lighthouse of Alexandria gave us the word “pharos”, meaning “lighthouse” in many languages. It also inspired lighthouse construction across the Mediterranean and beyond. It has became a symbol of navigation, innovation, and resilience.
