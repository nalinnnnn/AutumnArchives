MODERN ERA

“The future belongs to those who believe in the beauty of their dreams.” — Eleanor Roosevelt

An Age of Globalization, Technology, and Social Awakening

“The Modern Era is defined by rapid technological advancement, shifting cultural norms, and a growing focus on global connectivity, individual rights, and sustainability.”

KEY CONCEPT : TECHNOLOGY & IDENTITY

The digital age has redefined personal identity, community, and culture. Social media, artificial intelligence, and digital art have created new forms of expression and connection — while also raising questions about privacy, ethics, and authenticity.

3 ICONIC FIGURES OF THE MODERN ERA

Elon Musk

Known for: SpaceX, Tesla, and pushing technological boundaries
Musk represents the ambition of the modern tech era — from electric cars to space exploration — while sparking debates on innovation vs. controversy.

Malala Yousafzai

Known for: Advocacy for girls' education and global peace
Survivor, Nobel Laureate, and activist, Malala embodies the global fight for education, human rights, and resilience in the face of oppression.

Barack Obama

Known for: First African American President of the United States

Obama’s presidency (2009–2017) marked a historic milestone in U.S. and global politics. His leadership emphasized hope, inclusivity, diplomacy, and progress in areas such as healthcare reform, climate policy, and international cooperation.
