STATUE OF ZEUS

~It seems that Zeus has descended from Olympus to sit here in person.” — A visitor to Olympia, 2,000 years ago~

“If Jupiter were to descend to Earth, he would take the likeness of Zeus at Olympia.” — Strabo, ancient Greek geographer

By duncan1890

The statue was sculpted by Phidias, the most famous sculptor in ancient Greece, around 435 B.C. in Olympia, the site of the ancient Olympic Games. The statue was around 12 meters (40 feet) tall and made of ivory and gold (a technique called chryselephantine). It showed Zeus seated on a majestic throne, holding a statue of Nike (Victory) in his right hand and a scepter topped with an eagle in his left.

It was built to honor Zeus, the chief deity of the Greek pantheon, and to show the power and spiritual importance of Olympia as a sacred site. Sadly, the statue was destroyed sometime in the 5th century A.D., possibly in a fire. Today, only descriptions and depictions remain, but its fame lived on for centuries as a symbol of divine presence on Earth.

The Fall & Legacy ⚡

- Today there is nothing left of the mighty temple that housed the famous statue of Zeus in antiquity.

Though it no longer exists, it inspired later representations of Zeus and Jupiter in art and literature.

It remains a powerful symbol of classical Greek religious devotion and artistic genius.
